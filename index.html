About Me

A wise man once said, “You never change your life until you step out of your comfort zone; change begins at the end of your comfort zone.” — Roy T. Bennett. I wish I had heeded these words sooner. I am a man of principles, hard work, and commitment, driven by a clear purpose: to become the best version of myself in every aspect of life. I pursue this goal in my fitness, academics, and personal life by first stepping outside my comfort zone.

My journey has been complicated and lengthy. Since I was 10, I’ve moved frequently with my family, and even now, as I navigate life on my own, that pattern continues. This lifestyle has taught me to cherish every moment, even on my days off, because those moments may never come again. While this realization is significant, it can also be perilous, as I sometimes find myself living solely in the present without considering the consequences of my actions or my future. I believe it’s essential to strike a balance between enjoying the moment and planning ahead, and that pursuit of balance is my current life philosophy.

Outside of academics and philosophy, I’m a fitness enthusiast. This means I carefully cook, scale, and portion my meals, often planning what I’ll eat for the next week or two. Some might call it a bit obsessive, but I genuinely enjoy it. I work out at the gym, focusing on pushing my body to achieve an aesthetic look. In my free time, I also enjoy watching movies, shopping online, and exploring new food spots and coffee shops on the weekends. My favorite cuisines are Persian, Turkish, Japanese, and Italian.

For those looking to change their lives, try something new, or feeling scared to take that first step: just do it. You only live once, and opportunities don’t wait. Embrace the unknown, step outside your comfort zone, and take that leap. You’ll be surprised at what you can achieve when you start moving forward.
